Whether you're a student choosing a career path or you're a manager who wants to expand your skillset and leverage procurement and contract management to achieve your strategic goals, this graduate certificate can help open new doors and opportunities. Program Highlights Hands-on experience: Access to internships and other opportunities Networking benefits: Interact meaningfully with your professors and other public procurement and contract management professionals Knowledge, skills, and abilities: Develop field-specific capabilities through applied practices and concepts Managerial competencies in contracting: Engage critical thinking and analyses across all phases of the contract cycle Strategic orientation of public procurement: Convince policymakers and senior managers that effective procurement can support an entity’s strategic goals Procurement law and ethics: Acquire fundamental understanding of procurement and contracting legal and ethical principles Available In-Person and Online AT A GLANCE Strome College of Business School of Public Service Degree Level Graduate Degree Earned Certificate Delivery Modes Online On-Campus Careers Check out these ideas from ODU Career Development Services and the Occupational Information Network (O*NET). Students entering the Certificate program in Public Procurement & Contract Management should meet the minimum university admission requirements Graduate Admission. Certificate applicants enroll as Non-Degree Graduate Students thru the Office of Graduate Admissions. Please visit the Office of Graduate Admissions website. Applicants may also simultaneously apply to the Graduate Certificate program and the Master of Public Administration, or the Master of Business Administration. The course introduces the student to how properly-aligned, best practice acquisitions can support public entities' strategic goals. Challenges and opportunities for all stakeholders are addressed. Special attention is given to ongoing changes in public procurement. A strong emphasis is placed on the strategic role that procurement can play in public sector organizations and how procurement planning and source selection, in particular, fit into that role.
