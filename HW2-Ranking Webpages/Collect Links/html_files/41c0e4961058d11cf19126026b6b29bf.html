To be useful, this data must be analyzed and transformed into information and actionable knowledge. The graduate certificate in Business Analytics with Big Data provides students with business analytics knowledge and skills necessary in the modern data-centric business environment. Students learn to apply descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analytics techniques to business decision-making with an emphasis on predictive analytics and data analysis. In addition, students gain a working knowledge of data management methods. Program Highlights An understanding of analytic techniques and their application prepares students for success in data-focused professional environments that are becoming the norm. This certificate provides exposure to the “full spectrum” of analytics, including data management, data analysis, visualization, forecasting, and business modeling methods. More Program and Degree Requirements Featured Courses BNAL 415 View full course description for Advanced Business Analytics/Big Data Applications This course addresses advanced business analytics techniques and the application of such techniques to large data sets.… BNAL 415 Advanced Business Analytics/Big Data Applications This course addresses advanced business analytics techniques and the application of such techniques to large data sets. Some alternative business analytics strategies are introduced. Descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive models are included. Topics covered in this course include data visualization and exploration, cluster analysis, and developing and calibrating predictive models for big data. Applications of multivariate, logistic, and probit regression to business analytics are discussed. Software packages such as SAS/JMP/SPSS may be used. Prerequisites: A grade of C or better in BNAL 306 and a declared major in the University or permission from the Dean's Office.
