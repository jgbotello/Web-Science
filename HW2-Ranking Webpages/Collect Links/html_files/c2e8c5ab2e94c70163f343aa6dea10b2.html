The program consists of four graduate courses that expose students to supply chain management and logistics, transportation management, strategic sourcing and supply management, and distribution center management. Program Highlights ODU’s Maritime and Supply Chain Management programs provide continuing education that addresses the high demand for Supply Chain professionals in Hampton Roads and beyond This unique graduate certificate program is designed to help working professionals develop and sharpen their skills and advance their careers. You will be exposed to current topics in: Supply Chain Management and Logistics, Strategic Procurement and Supply Management, Transportation Management, and Distribution Center Management ODU is the only Hampton Roads university with a Graduate Supply Chain Certificate Management program, and the only U.S. university that offers Maritime and Supply Chain Management undergraduate and graduate programs. More Program and Degree Requirements Featured Courses MSCM 641 View full course description for Supply Chain Management and Logistics Supply chain management integrates all activities associated with the flows of materials, information, and funds.… MSCM 641 Supply Chain Management and Logistics Supply chain management integrates all activities associated with the flows of materials, information, and funds. Examples include strategic sourcing, order processing, warehousing, inventory management, transportation and logistics, and the costs and information systems supporting these activities. MSCM 530 View full course description for Strategic Sourcing and Purchasing Management An overview of the strategic sourcing of materials and services in the organization and its role in the supply chain.… MSCM 530 Strategic Sourcing and Purchasing Management An overview of the strategic sourcing of materials and services in the organization and its role in the supply chain. Topics include sourcing decisions, price/cost analysis, quality issues, purchasing, supplier selection, legal and ethical issues, third party logistics, freight forwarding, and acquisition of services and capital assets. MSCM 568 View full course description for Distribution Center and Material Handling Management This course is designed to investigate the strategic role of distribution center and material management in the supply… MSCM 568 Distribution Center and Material Handling Management This course is designed to investigate the strategic role of distribution center and material management in the supply chain. Course content includes the analysis of distribution center operations through the study of design, system selection, and layout configuration as well as the evaluation of material handling and inventory management options.
