She received her Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Michigan State University, USA. Her expertise is in cardiovascular mechanics, and her research aims to characterize cardiovascular tissues and systems from a mechanical point of view to improve our understanding of health and disease. She is interested in the development of biodevices and biomaterials for the diagnosis, treatment, and prediction of disease and injury.Kim is an assistant professor in the Department of Smart Bio-Industrial Mechanical Engineering at Kyungpook National University, South Korea.
