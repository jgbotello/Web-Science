About this Program

The applied mathematics major is intended for students wishing to pursue graduate work in mathematics or otherwise obtain employment in a mathematics field.




Program Highlights


A variety of 400-level classes that apply the mathematics developed within the Math Core courses


Required classes in theoretical mathematics to develop a deeper understanding of the rigorous nature of mathematics development


Computational math classes to teach mathematical computer modeling

        MATH 317
            



Calculus IV: Introductory Analysis





An introduction to real analysis. Topics covered include completeness and topological properties of the real line, theory of sequences, limits of functions, the derivative, the Riemann integral, and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. Prerequisites: A grade of C or better in MATH 212.


















        MATH 408
            




View full course description for 
Applied Numerical Methods I



                  An introduction to the numerical methods commonly used by scientists and engineers. Topics include solutions of…
              











        MATH 408
            



Applied Numerical Methods I





An introduction to the numerical methods commonly used by scientists and engineers. Topics include solutions of equations of one variable, direct methods for solving linear systems, matrix factorization, stability analysis, iterative techniques, polynomial interpolation, numerical differentiation and integration, approximation theory, and initial and boundary value problems for ordinary differential equations. Prerequisites: A grade of C or better in MATH 316; CS 150 or equivalent programming ability also required.


















        MATH 420

                  Exploring mathematical models in various biological contexts using both difference and differential equations: single…
              











        MATH 420
            



Applied Mathematics I: Biomathematics





Exploring mathematical models in various biological contexts using both difference and differential equations: single and multiple species population growth, predator-prey and competing species (using phase plane analysis), epidemiological models of epidemics and pandemics, tumor growth, pattern formation in animals and insects. Prerequisites: A grade of C or better in MATH 307.
