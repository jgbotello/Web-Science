Nuclear Medicine Technology (B.S.N.M.T.) Program HighlightsCareersCoursesRequirementsTransferCostNext-StepsContact Request Info Apply About this Program Nuclear medicine technology is the medical specialty that utilizes sealed and unsealed radioactive materials in the diagnosis and treatment of disease. The nuclear medicine technology program at Old Dominion University is designed to prepare individuals as entry-level nuclear medicine technologists. Program Highlights One of 75 nuclear medicine technology programs in the United States, and one of only 33 baccalaureate degree granting programs. Medical and Health Services Managers $111,680 Median Salary Plan, direct, or coordinate medical and health services in hospitals, clinics, managed care organizations, public health agencies, or similar organizations. Nuclear Medicine Technologists $77,660 Median Salary Prepare, administer, and measure radioactive isotopes in therapeutic, diagnostic, and tracer studies using a variety of radioisotope equipment. Prepare stock solutions of radioactive materials and calculate doses to be administered by radiologists. Subject patients to radiation. Execute blood volume, red cell survival, and fat absorption studies following standard laboratory techniques. The methods and mathematics… NMED 331 Fundamental Concepts in Nuclear Medicine Technology A course designed to cover the physical principles related to nuclear medicine technology. The methods and mathematics of radioactive decay, types of radiation, radiation interactions, origins of radionuclides, including SPECT and PET/CT radionuclides also presented. Prerequisites: PHYS 101N and PHYS 102N or equivalent. NMED 335 View full course description for Radiation Health Discussions of radiation effects on cellular systems as well as guidelines for radiation protection and safe handling… NMED 335 Radiation Health Discussions of radiation effects on cellular systems as well as guidelines for radiation protection and safe handling of radioactive materials in the nuclear medicine clinical setting. Prerequisites: NMED 331 or permission of the instructor. NMED 401 View full course description for Nuclear Medicine Technology I A course designed to cover the nuclear medicine procedures and protocols of the gastrointestinal, genitourinary,… NMED 401 Nuclear Medicine Technology I A course designed to cover the nuclear medicine procedures and protocols of the gastrointestinal, genitourinary, central nervous, skeletal systems. Other current or emerging clinical nuclear medicine procedures are also covered. Prerequisites: BIOL 240 or BIOL 250 and BIOL 241 or BIOL 251 and admission to the nuclear medicine program.
