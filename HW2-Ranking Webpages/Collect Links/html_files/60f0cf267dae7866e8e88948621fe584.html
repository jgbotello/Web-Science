Our Mission Breadcrumb Home Social Mobility Social Mobility Social mobility in higher education refers to the ability of individuals, particularly those from underrepresented backgrounds, to improve their socioeconomic status through educational opportunities. It encompasses the process of moving from a lower income or social class to a higher one by acquiring education, skills, and opportunities that enable personal and financial growth. Old Dominion University (ODU) is committed to promoting social mobility through various initiatives that support students from diverse backgrounds in achieving academic success and advancing their futures.   Social Mobility Home About Events Past Events Initiatives Research Rankings Symposium Videos Research Research Rankings Rankings Videos Videos Social Mobility Initiatives First Star Old Dominion University Academy Check out what ODU is providing for our foster youth. First Star's Foster Youth Academies are the country's first long-term college-prep programs for high school foster youth that include four immersive residential summers on a university campus and monthly sessions during each school year. Brother 2 Brother (B2B) Brother 2 Brother is a SEES initiative dedicated specifically to assist African American, and Latinx men of color to excel academically, socially, culturally, and professionally. First-Generation @ ODU First-Generation @ ODU aims to support students who are the first in their families to attend college. These initiatives are tailored to provide a range of resources to help first-generation students succeed academically, socially, and emotionally.
