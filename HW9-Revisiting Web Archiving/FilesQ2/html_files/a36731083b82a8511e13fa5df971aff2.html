Creative Writing (M.F.A.) As part of the English Department at ODU, the MFA Program began in 1994 with twelve students and three creative writing instructors. Criminology & Criminal Justice (Ph.D.) The Ph.D. in Criminology and Criminal Justice is a sociological criminology program that highlights social inequality and public policy in the study of crime and justice issues. English (M.A.) The Master of Arts program in English develops professional competency in literary and textual analysis and in writing. English (Ph.D.) The Ph.D. in English is an innovative program that integrates digital humanities, writing studies, literature, rhetoric, discourse, and cultural studies, offering opportunities for creative reinterpretation of these fields within the discipline of English. History (M.A.) The Department of History offers a Master of Arts degree that prepares competent graduates for modern careers in business, government, teaching, non-profit work, and further graduate study in history, law, library science, or business. Humanities (M.A.) The interdisciplinary Masters' degree (MA) program in the Humanities allows students to pursue individualized curricula that incorporate classes from across ODU's seven Colleges and 65 standalone graduate programs. International Studies (M.A.) The M.A. in International Studies is a highly customizable and interdisciplinary degree. The Graduate Program in International Studies (GPIS) offers advanced research opportunities and training in global problems and transnational issues. International Studies (Ph.D.) The Ph.D. in International Studies is a highly customizable and interdisciplinary degree. The Graduate Program in International Studies (GPIS) offers advanced research opportunities and training in global problems and transnational issues. Lifespan & Digital Communication (M.A.) ODU’s graduate degree program in Lifespan & Digital Communication is distinctive in the field of communication. Music Education (M.M.E.) The Master of Music Education degree is designed to stress the development of advanced knowledge of broad-based principles and practices in music and music education for application in the public or private school, private studio, or higher education setting.
